# Project Report: Talk2Mind — A Multimodal AI-Based Mental Well-Being Assessment and Support System

## 1. Problem Statement

Mental health issues such as stress, anxiety, depression, sleep disorders, and emotional burnout
have increased significantly in recent years. A large percentage of employees and students
experience mental health challenges but remain unaware of their symptoms until the condition
becomes severe. Traditional mental health assessments often require consultations with
professionals, making early self-assessment difficult and inaccessible for many individuals.

**Objective:** Develop an AI-powered mental well-being assessment system that analyzes a
person's emotional state through visual and speech-based interactions, enabling early
identification of potential mental health concerns and providing personalized recommendations.

## 2. Methodology

Talk2Mind acts as an intelligent virtual mental wellness assistant. Users interact through a
guided conversation session (~10–15 minutes), during which the system collects information from
four sources:

1. **Facial Expression Analysis** — computer-vision-derived emotion probabilities and
   micro-expression signals (eye contact ratio, blink rate, facial muscle tension, smile
   frequency, head movement variance).
2. **Speech Emotion Analysis** — voice/audio-derived features (MFCC-style embeddings, pitch,
   energy, speech rate, pause ratio, jitter, shimmer).
3. **Questionnaire-Based Assessment** — a PHQ-9 + GAD-7 inspired self-report questionnaire
   capturing depression- and anxiety-related indicators.
4. **Behavioral Feature Fusion** — combining all three modalities into one representation.

Fused features are passed through a scoring engine (rule-based weighted sum, and a learned MLP
regressor) to produce a **Mental Well-Being Score (0–100)** and a **risk category**
(Good / Mild Concern / Moderate Concern / High Concern). The score then drives a **personalized
recommendation engine**.

## 3. Dataset

Because collecting real facial video, audio, and questionnaire responses from human participants
requires informed consent and ethics review, this phase of the project uses a **synthetic,
label-consistent dataset** generated by `data/generate_dataset.py`. A hidden per-user latent
distress variable (drawn from a Beta(2,5) distribution to reflect a realistic population skew —
most people report low-to-moderate distress, with a smaller tail of high distress) drives
correlated facial, speech, and questionnaire signals, so the relationships between modalities
resemble what would be expected in real subjects.

## 4. Model Development

### 4.1 Facial Emotion Recognition
A RandomForestClassifier trained on the facial feature set (visual embedding + engineered
signals) predicts the dominant facial emotion. In production, this would be replaced by a CNN
backbone (ResNet/EfficientNet) fine-tuned on FER2013/AffectNet-style data, run frame-by-frame on
webcam video and pooled at the session level.

### 4.2 Speech Emotion Recognition
A GradientBoostingClassifier trained on the speech feature set (MFCC-style embedding + acoustic
features) predicts the dominant speech emotion. In production, this would be replaced by a
BiLSTM/Transformer model trained on MFCCs extracted via `librosa` from raw audio (e.g.
RAVDESS/CREMA-D-style training data).

### 4.3 Multimodal Feature Fusion
Facial and speech feature vectors, plus questionnaire scores, are concatenated and standardized,
then fed into a small MLP regressor (`src/feature_fusion.py`) trained to predict the Mental
Well-Being Score directly — this lets the model learn data-driven modality weights rather than
fixed hand-set ones.

### 4.4 Mental Well-Being Scoring Engine
Two interchangeable scoring modes are provided:
- **Rule-based** (`rule_based_score`): a transparent weighted sum of questionnaire, facial, and
  speech "distress proxies" (default weights: 50% questionnaire, 25% facial, 25% speech).
- **Model-based** (`ModelBasedScorer`): wraps the trained fusion MLP for a learned score.

### 4.5 Personalized Recommendation Engine
Given the risk category, specific high-scoring questionnaire items, and elevated
facial/speech-distress signals, the engine returns a short, prioritized list of self-care
suggestions. If a crisis indicator is detected (self-harm question ≥ 1), the engine always
surfaces a crisis-support message ahead of generic tips.

## 6. Dashboard

`dashboard/app.py` is a Streamlit application where users complete the questionnaire, adjust
sliders simulating facial/speech signals (standing in for live webcam/mic capture), and instantly
see their Mental Well-Being Score, risk category, and personalized recommendations, plus a
progress-over-time chart.

## 7. Limitations & Ethical Considerations

- **Synthetic data**: current model performance reflects a synthetic, label-consistent dataset
  and must be re-validated on real, consented data before any real-world use.
- **Not a diagnostic tool**: Talk2Mind is a self-awareness aid, not a replacement for
  professional clinical assessment.
- **Bias & fairness**: real deployment requires auditing across age, gender, accent, and cultural
  expression-of-emotion differences, since facial/speech emotion expression varies across
  populations.
- **Privacy**: facial video and voice are sensitive biometric data; real deployment needs
  explicit consent, secure storage, encryption, and a clear data-retention policy.
- **Crisis handling**: any system like this needs a clear, tested escalation path to human
  support for high-risk/crisis cases — it should never be the only safety net.
